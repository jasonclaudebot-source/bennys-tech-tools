### Actually Useful Buying Guide

Don't get lost in the marketing BS. Here's what matters:

**Screen Refresh Rate:**
- 60Hz = Fine if you're casual, boring if you're serious
- 144Hz = The sweet spot. Noticeably smoother
- 165Hz+ = Overkill for most people, but feels amazing

**Graphics Card (GPU):**
- **RTX 4060** = Plays 2024 games fine at 1080p. Acceptable minimum
- **RTX 4070** = 1440p high settings. Real gaming territory
- **RTX 4080+** = "I want max settings on everything" energy

**Processor (CPU):**
- New Intel (i7/i9) or AMD Ryzen 7/9 = Don't cheap out here
- Older processors = Games will still run, just slower

**RAM:**
- 16GB = Enough for gaming + some other stuff
- 32GB = If you're streaming or doing video editing alongside gaming

**Storage:**
- 512GB SSD minimum (barely), 1TB+ is real talk
- You'll fill that up faster than you think

**The Temperature Thing:**
Most gaming laptops run hot. It's just how it is. Thermal pads exist for a reason. This isn't a bug, it's a feature.

### What Actually Matters

**Gaming laptops are heavy.** Like, "did I just get bicep gains carrying this" heavy. If you're moving it around a lot, think about that.

**Battery life is a myth.** Seriously. You're getting 4-6 hours if you're lucky and not gaming. It's fine, just accept it.

**Cooling pads help.** If you're going to game for hours, get one. Your laptop (and your lap) will thank you.
